    • Selected Projects


  • Sublime Spelling and Grammar Check Plugin

    May 2017–Present

    Working on creating a comprehensive, quick, Microsoft Word-esque spelling and grammar checker as a plugin for Sublime Text. Many people in the computer science community extensively use Sublime as a text editor, so I believe such a tool could be of use. Following test-driven development.

  • Predictive NBA Classifier

    January 2017–May 2017

    Wrote a paper on using machine learning tools to predict the results of NBA games (classification) and the margin of victory (regression). Utilized extensive feature space design, parameter tuning, and data scraping.

  • Automated World Generation

    May 2017–Present

    Iterative algorithm-based project (based on my work on the project below) to create a more comprehensive, realistic world generation algorithm than ones currently available. Based on two-opt polygon generation, machine-learning based rules for biome generation.

  • Three Dimensional Terrain Generator

    May 2014–March 2015

    Worked on more optimized, more realistic terrain generation based on improvements to the diamond-square algorithm. Created in Java and demonstrated in JMonkeyEngine.

    What I'm Listening To Right Now

    OK Computer OKNOTOK 1997 2017

    Radiohead

    In the pantheon of rock albums, OK Computer has definitively carved its place as one of the chief deities, an album that was both ahead of its time yet firmly rooted in the present, quiet and subtle but roaring with emotion, filled with hits but continually pushing the genre in new and innovative directions. It's no wonder Radiohead were seen as heroes back in the day.

    It's always worth going back and listening to this gem of an album, but with an entire disk's worth of songs that the band somehow deemed not worth releasing at the time (with songs like I Promise??? What were they thinking???), it's certainly worth a listen, even if you aren't a diehard Radiohead fan.
